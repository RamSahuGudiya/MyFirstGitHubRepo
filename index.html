A password, sometimes called a passcode, is a memorized secret, typically a string of characters, used to confirm the identity of a user. Using the terminology of the NIST Digital Identity Guidelines, the secret is memorized by a party called the claimant while the party verifying the identity of the claimant is called the verifier.
